## Summary

Two distinct "AI" angles must be kept separate, because conflating them is the gimmick trap. (a) ML INSIDE the cache: the learned-caching literature is real and has measured wins, but almost entirely off the hot path or in latency-tolerant domains (CDN, flash, CPU-microarchitecture simulators), NOT in microsecond-budget in-memory KV serving. LRB (NSDI'20, Song et al.) uses a gradient-boosted decision tree (LightGBM-class) to approximate "Relaxed Belady" — predicting which objects fall beyond a Belady boundary — and cuts CDN WAN traffic 4-25% vs a production design, with explicitly engineered lightweight training/inference and bounded memory. LeCaR (HotStorage'18) uses online regret-minimization over just two experts (LRU+LFU) and beats ARC by up to ~18x at very small cache/working-set ratios. CACHEUS (FAST'21) generalizes this to an adaptive mixture of experts (SR-LRU + CR-LFU) chosen per workload primitive (LFU/LRU/scan/churn). Parrot (ICML'20) is a neural (attention-based) imitation-learning approximation of Belady that cuts CPU-cache miss rate ~20% on SPEC and raises web-search hit rate ~61% over LRU — but it is a research simulator, explicitly not hot-path-deployable (NN inference per access). Baleen (FAST'24) applies ML to flash-cache ADMISSION + prefetching using an "episodes" residency model, cutting peak disk-head time ~12% and TCO ~17%.

(b) The hot-path cost problem is the crux for IronCache. The "Can Increasing the Hit Ratio Hurt Cache Throughput?" analysis (ValueTools'24 / Queueing Systems'26, Harchol-Balter et al.) shows that for LRU/SLRU the bottleneck is the per-hit list-relink (delinkT) under lock contention, so chasing hit ratio can REDUCE throughput; FIFO alone monotonically improves throughput with hit ratio. SIEVE (NSDI'24, best paper) and S3-FIFO show that lazy promotion + quick demotion on FIFO-style queues beats LRU on both hit ratio (SIEVE cuts FIFO miss ratio ~21% mean) and thread scalability, with <20 LOC integration. The lesson for a max-throughput-per-core Rust cache: any per-request ML inference is a non-starter; ML belongs OFF the hot path as a background advisor that retunes cheap, deterministic policy knobs (eviction policy choice, sample count, LFU decay, slab/encoding thresholds, per-value compression decisions), with the hot path remaining a branch-free, allocation-free, lock-light O(1) policy. Note Redis itself already rejects "true" anything: defaults are maxmemory-policy=noeviction, sampled approximate LRU/LFU (maxmemory-samples=5, a 5-bit Morris-counter LFU with lfu-log-factor=10, lfu-decay-time=1) — explicitly trading exactness for memory/CPU. IronCache should beat this with W-TinyLFU-style admission (Caffeine: 4-bit count-min sketch, ~8 bytes/entry, near-Belady hit rate) as the non-ML SOTA floor, then use ML only to pick/tune among such cheap policies.

(b') AI-assisted DEVELOPMENT of IronCache is the more immediately credible "AI" story and is the project's stated method. The right framing: LLM/agent pipelines mine prior art (this very task), propose candidate mechanisms, AUTO-GENERATE benchmarks/traces and adversarially verify quantitative claims before they enter design docs, and gate experiments. The discipline must be: every numeric claim pinned to a primary source + version, an adversarial verifier agent that tries to falsify each claim and a benchmark harness that reproduces it on real traces (Wikipedia/MSR/Twitter Cluster/SocialNetwork) before any policy is accepted. This is the safe, non-gimmick AI: it improves the engineering process and the rigor of claims, with humans gating merges, rather than putting a model on the data path.

Net stance: BORROW the off-hot-path "advisor" pattern (LeCaR/CACHEUS-style expert selection, LRB-style background reuse-distance signal) and ADAPT it to retune deterministic knobs; REJECT per-request neural inference (Parrot-class) on the hot path; treat W-TinyLFU + SIEVE/S3-FIFO as the strong non-ML baselines the advisor switches between; and BORROW the LLM-agent verification harness as core project infrastructure, with hard guardrails (deterministic replay, adversarial fact-check, human merge gate).

## Mechanisms: borrow, adapt, or reject

| Mechanism | System | Stance | What it does | Rationale for IronCache |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| GBT reuse-distance prediction (LRB / Learning Relaxed Belady) | LRB (NSDI'20) | **adapt** | A gradient-boosted decision tree predicts whether an object's next request lies beyond a 'Belady boundary'; objects predicted far-future are evicted. Training data and inference are engineered to be lightweight with bounded memory. | The PREDICTION SIGNAL (approximate reuse distance) is valuable, but tree inference per eviction candidate is too heavy for a microsecond hot path. Adapt: run a background GBT/online model that periodically emits cheap per-class or per-slab scores the hot path consumes as a static threshold; never call the model per request. |
| Regret-minimizing expert mixture (LeCaR) | LeCaR (HotStorage'18) | **borrow** | Maintains weights over two eviction experts (LRU, LFU), samples the eviction from the weighted policies, and updates weights via regret minimization on observed misses (using a history/ghost list). | This is the ideal off-hot-path 'advisor' primitive: O(1), no neural net, tiny state, provably adaptive. IronCache can run a LeCaR-style controller in a background thread to pick the active hot-path policy (SIEVE vs W-TinyLFU vs LRU-sampled) per shard/workload. |
| Adaptive expert selection by workload primitive (CACHEUS) | CACHEUS (FAST'21) | **adapt** | Classifies the workload into LFU/LRU/scan/churn primitives and adaptively blends scan-resistant (SR-LRU) and churn-resistant (CR-LFU) experts. | Borrow the workload-primitive taxonomy as the advisor's feature space, but reject the heavier expert blending on the hot path; instead map a detected primitive to one cheap deterministic policy + knob set. |
| Neural imitation of Belady (Parrot) | Parrot (ICML'20) | **reject** | An attention-based neural net trained by imitation learning approximates the optimal Belady eviction decision from past-access features. | Per-access neural inference is fundamentally incompatible with max-throughput-per-core and determinism. Useful only as an OFFLINE oracle/ceiling to measure how much hit-ratio headroom a cheap policy is leaving on the table. |
| ML flash admission + prefetch with 'episodes' (Baleen) | Baleen (FAST'24) | **adapt** | Groups accesses into residency episodes and trains ML admission/prefetch models optimized for a TCO/disk-head-time metric rather than raw hit ratio. | IronCache is in-memory, so flash-specific admission is out of scope, but the 'optimize the real cost metric, not hit ratio' insight and the episode-based offline training data construction directly inform IronCache's optional disk/SSD tier and its advisor's objective function. |
| W-TinyLFU admission via 4-bit Count-Min Sketch | Caffeine / TinyLFU (ACM TOS'17) | **borrow** | A frequency sketch (4-bit counters, ~8 bytes/entry, aged) gates admission so a one-hit-wonder cannot evict a high-frequency resident; small LRU window absorbs recency. | This is the non-ML SOTA: near-Belady hit ratio, deterministic, ~8 bytes/entry, no inference. IronCache should implement this as the default admission filter and the baseline any ML advisor must beat before it's allowed to change anything. |
| Lazy promotion / quick demotion (SIEVE, S3-FIFO) | SIEVE (NSDI'24), S3-FIFO | **borrow** | FIFO queue with a hand that retains recently-accessed objects and evicts the rest, avoiding the per-hit relink that bottlenecks LRU; quick demotion drops new objects fast. | Directly serves the throughput-per-core goal: avoids the delinkT lock contention that the queueing analysis identifies, scales across threads, <20 LOC. Strong default hot-path eviction structure. |
| Sampled approximate LRU/LFU with Morris counters | Redis | **adapt** | Samples maxmemory-samples (default 5) keys per eviction and evicts the worst; LFU uses a logarithmic Morris counter with time decay instead of exact counts. | Borrow the 'approximate, sampled, fixed-memory' philosophy and make sample count an ADVISOR-TUNED knob (per workload, trade accuracy vs CPU). Reject noeviction as the default for a cache product — IronCache should default to a real eviction policy. |
| RL/Bayesian knob autotuning (CDBTune / OtterTune) | CDBTune (SIGMOD'19), OtterTune (SIGMOD'17) | **adapt** | CDBTune uses a DDPG RL agent (state = internal metrics, action = knob deltas, reward = throughput/latency delta) and OtterTune uses Gaussian-Process surrogate models to recommend DB configurations. | Borrow the closed-loop autotuning pattern for IronCache's background advisor (tune maxmemory-samples, LFU decay, slab/encoding/compression thresholds), but reject heavy RL training online; prefer cheap Bayesian/bandit optimization with safe rollback and bounded knob ranges. |
| LLM-agent prior-art mining + adversarial claim verification | AI-assisted development (this project's method) | **borrow** | Agent pipeline fans out web/source searches, extracts claims pinned to primary sources/versions, runs an adversarial verifier that tries to falsify each number, and auto-generates benchmark/trace replays to reproduce claimed gains before a mechanism is accepted. | This is the credible, safe 'AI' for IronCache: it raises engineering rigor without putting a model on the data path. Guardrails: deterministic trace replay, human merge gate, every claim carries source_url + version + confidence. |

## Implications for IronCache

- Hot path stays ML-free and deterministic. Default eviction = a SIEVE/S3-FIFO-style FIFO-with-reinsertion structure (lazy promotion, quick demotion) to avoid the per-hit relink contention that the ValueTools'24 queueing analysis shows can make higher hit ratio LOWER throughput. No neural inference per request, ever.
- Default admission = W-TinyLFU (4-bit Count-Min Sketch, ~8 bytes/entry, periodic aging). This is the non-ML SOTA floor; ship it before any ML and require any advisor change to beat it on replayed traces.
- Ship a real eviction default, not Redis's noeviction. Pick an allkeys policy by default (likely SIEVE+W-TinyLFU admission) since IronCache is positioned as a cache, while still supporting a noeviction mode for store-of-record users.
- ML lives only in an off-hot-path 'advisor' thread. It observes counters (hit ratio, miss latency, scan/churn signals, eviction age) and retunes a small set of bounded, deterministic knobs: active policy choice, sample count (Redis-style), LFU log-factor/decay, slab/encoding thresholds, and per-value compression decisions. Hot path reads a published config snapshot atomically; no per-request model calls.
- Advisor controller should be a LeCaR/CACHEUS-style expert-selection mechanism (regret minimization or a contextual bandit over a handful of cheap policies), per shard, with ghost-list accounting. This gives adaptivity with O(1) cost and no GPU/heavy-model dependency in the single static binary.
- Per-value compression decision is a natural, low-risk first ML target: a cheap learned/heuristic classifier off the hot path decides whether a value class is worth compressing (and with which codec/level), set as a threshold the hot path applies deterministically. Measure CPU-per-byte vs memory-saved, not just ratio.
- Treat learned-Belady (LRB GBT, Parrot NN) strictly as OFFLINE ceilings in the benchmark harness to quantify how much hit-ratio headroom cheap policies leave; do not ship them on the path. This keeps the 'AI' honest and measurable.
- Optimize the right metric, per Baleen: the advisor's objective should be throughput-per-core and memory efficiency (and, for any disk tier, backend load/TCO), NOT raw hit ratio in isolation — directly aligned with IronCache's stated goal.
- Build the AI-assisted development harness as first-class project infra: every prior-art claim pinned to a primary source + upstream version + confidence; an adversarial verifier agent that tries to falsify each number; auto-generated benchmark replays on public traces (Wikipedia, MSR Cambridge, Twitter Cluster, SocialNetwork) gating acceptance; humans gate merges. No agent-proposed mechanism enters a design doc without a reproduced measurement.
- Safety guardrails for the advisor: bounded knob ranges, hysteresis/cooldown to prevent oscillation, automatic rollback on throughput/hit-ratio regression, and a hard kill-switch to a known-good static config — the cache must be fully correct and fast with the advisor disabled.

## Research papers and primary sources

- **Learning Relaxed Belady for Content Distribution Network Caching** (NSDI 2020). A gradient-boosted decision tree approximates Belady by predicting whether an object's next access falls beyond a 'Belady boundary'; engineered for lightweight training/inference and bounded memory, deployed in an Apache Traffic Server prototype. [source](https://www.usenix.org/system/files/nsdi20-paper-song.pdf) Relevance: Canonical 'learned eviction that actually shipped a prototype'; the 4-25% WAN reduction shows the ceiling, and its engineering of off-path training informs IronCache's advisor design.
- **Driving Cache Replacement with ML-based LeCaR** (HotStorage 2018). Online regret minimization over two experts (LRU, LFU) with ghost lists; beats ARC by up to 18x at small cache/working-set ratios. [source](https://www.usenix.org/conference/hotstorage18/presentation/vietri) Relevance: The minimal, O(1), no-neural-net advisor primitive IronCache should adopt for background policy selection.
- **Learning Cache Replacement with CACHEUS** (FAST 2021). Adaptive mixture of experts (SR-LRU scan-resistant, CR-LFU churn-resistant) selected per workload primitive (LFU/LRU/scan/churn) across 17,766 experiments. [source](https://www.usenix.org/system/files/fast21-rodriguez.pdf) Relevance: Provides the workload-primitive taxonomy and adaptivity model for IronCache's advisor feature space.
- **An Imitation Learning Approach for Cache Replacement (Parrot)** (ICML 2020). Attention-based neural net imitates Belady from past accesses; ~20% miss-rate reduction on SPEC, ~61% hit-rate increase over LRU on web search. [source](http://proceedings.mlr.press/v119/liu20f/liu20f.pdf) Relevance: Defines the hit-ratio ceiling and demonstrates exactly why per-access neural inference is NOT hot-path-deployable — the boundary IronCache must respect.
- **Baleen: ML Admission & Prefetching for Flash Caches** (FAST 2024). 'Episodes' residency model for training; ML admission + prefetch optimized for disk-head-time/TCO, ~12% peak disk-head time and ~17% TCO reduction. [source](https://www.usenix.org/system/files/fast24-wong.pdf) Relevance: Teaches 'optimize the real cost metric, not hit ratio' and an offline training-data construction method relevant to any IronCache disk tier and the advisor objective.
- **TinyLFU: A Highly Efficient Cache Admission Policy** (ACM Transactions on Storage 2017). Approximate-LFU admission via a compact aging Count-Min Sketch (4-bit counters), with a doorkeeper to filter one-hit-wonders; W-TinyLFU adds a small LRU window. [source](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3149371) Relevance: The non-ML SOTA admission policy and the deterministic baseline IronCache should ship and benchmark every ML idea against.
- **SIEVE is Simpler than LRU: an Efficient Turn-Key Eviction Algorithm for Web Caches** (NSDI 2024 (best paper)). FIFO queue with a skipping hand (lazy promotion / quick demotion) avoids per-hit relinking; ~21% mean FIFO miss-ratio reduction with better thread scalability, <20 LOC integration. [source](https://www.usenix.org/system/files/nsdi24-zhang-yazhuo.pdf) Relevance: Strong default hot-path eviction structure that directly serves throughput-per-core and sidesteps the LRU relink bottleneck.
- **Can Increasing the Hit Ratio Hurt Cache Throughput?** (ValueTools 2024 / Queueing Systems 2026). Queueing model + measurement show LRU/SLRU per-hit operations are a contended bottleneck, so higher hit ratio can reduce throughput; FIFO does not have this pathology. [source](https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~harchol/Papers/ValueTools24.pdf) Relevance: The load-bearing argument for keeping the hot path cheap and FIFO-like and for measuring throughput, not just hit ratio — the core tension in IronCache's design.
- **An End-to-End Automatic Cloud Database Tuning System using Deep RL (CDBTune)** (SIGMOD 2019). DDPG reinforcement-learning agent tunes DB knobs from internal-metric state with a throughput/latency reward, fine-tuned online. [source](https://dbgroup.cs.tsinghua.edu.cn/ligl/papers/sigmod19-cdbtune.pdf) Relevance: Reference architecture for IronCache's background knob autotuner; informs the choice of cheaper bandit/Bayesian methods over full online RL.

## Open questions

- What is the measured marginal hit-ratio gain of a LeCaR/CACHEUS-style advisor OVER a well-tuned W-TinyLFU + SIEVE baseline on in-memory KV traces (Twitter Cluster, Meta), and is that gain large enough to justify the advisor's complexity and the risk of mis-tuning?
- Can the advisor retune knobs frequently enough to track workload shifts without causing throughput oscillation, and what hysteresis/cooldown and rollback policy guarantees no regression below the static baseline?
- What is the actual per-request budget on the hot path (ns) at IronCache's target throughput-per-core, and does even a single sketch lookup + branch for admission fit within it across value-size distributions?
- For per-value compression decisions, can a cheap offline-trained classifier beat a simple size/entropy heuristic enough to matter, and how is the decision encoded so the hot path stays branch-predictable?
- How do we construct a trustworthy benchmark/trace corpus (which public traces, what mix) so that adversarially verified claims actually generalize to IronCache's target deployments rather than overfitting to CDN/flash traces?
- What are the determinism and reproducibility requirements for the advisor (seeded, snapshot-versioned config) so that the cache's behavior is debuggable and a given input replay yields identical eviction decisions?
- Where exactly is the human-in-the-loop merge gate in the AI-assisted-development pipeline, and what is the minimum reproduction bar (re-run on N traces, verifier sign-off) before an agent-proposed mechanism can enter the design tree?

## Proposed issues (seeds for the tracker)

- **[decision, M0]** DECISION: Hot path is ML-free and deterministic; ML only off-path as a knob advisor. Adopt the principle that no per-request ML inference runs on the data path. ML is confined to a background advisor that retunes bounded, deterministic knobs and selects among cheap policies. Capture rationale (throughput-per-core, determinism, single-binary, the hit-ratio-vs-throughput tradeoff).
- **[research, M0]** RESEARCH: Quantify advisor headroom over a tuned W-TinyLFU + SIEVE baseline on in-memory KV traces. Replay public traces (Twitter Cluster, Meta/CacheLib, MSR, Wikipedia) to measure how much hit ratio a LeCaR/CACHEUS-style advisor adds over a well-tuned non-ML baseline, to decide whether the advisor is worth building.
- **[design, M1]** DESIGN: Background advisor architecture (expert selection + bounded knob autotuning). Specify a per-shard advisor thread: LeCaR/contextual-bandit expert selection over {SIEVE, W-TinyLFU-admission, sampled-LRU/LFU}, plus CDBTune/OtterTune-style tuning of sample count, LFU log-factor/decay, slab/encoding/compression thresholds, with atomic config snapshots consumed by the hot path.
- **[design, M1]** DESIGN: W-TinyLFU admission filter as the default non-ML baseline. Design the default admission policy: aging 4-bit Count-Min Sketch (~8 bytes/entry) + small LRU window + doorkeeper, as the deterministic SOTA floor every ML idea must beat before acceptance.
- **[design, M1]** DESIGN: SIEVE/S3-FIFO-style hot-path eviction structure. Specify a FIFO-with-reinsertion eviction core (lazy promotion / quick demotion) that avoids per-hit list relinking and scales across threads, addressing the documented LRU throughput pathology.
- **[design, M2]** DESIGN: Off-path per-value compression decision model. Design a cheap classifier (heuristic or offline-trained) that decides whether/how to compress a value class, emitting a deterministic threshold the hot path applies; objective is CPU-per-byte vs memory-saved, not ratio alone.
- **[non-goal, M0]** DECISION: Reject per-request neural inference (Parrot-class) on the data path. Record as an explicit non-goal that learned-Belady neural policies run on the hot path; they are permitted only as offline oracles/ceilings in the benchmark harness.
- **[task, M2]** TASK: Build the offline learned-Belady oracle/ceiling into the benchmark harness. Implement Belady-MIN and an LRB-style GBT oracle in the simulator to quantify the hit-ratio headroom cheap policies leave on each trace, without any production-path dependency.
- **[epic, M0]** EPIC: AI-assisted development pipeline with adversarial claim verification. Stand up the LLM/agent pipeline that mines prior art, proposes mechanisms, auto-generates benchmark replays, and runs an adversarial verifier on every numeric claim, with a human merge gate. This is core project infrastructure, not a cache feature.
- **[design, M1]** DESIGN: Adversarial claim-verification protocol (source-pinning, versioning, reproduction bar). Define the verification contract: every claim carries source_url + upstream version + confidence; a verifier agent attempts falsification; mechanisms require reproduced measurements on N traces before entering the design tree.
- **[design, M1]** DESIGN: Advisor safety guardrails (bounded knobs, hysteresis, rollback, kill-switch). Specify safety: bounded knob ranges, oscillation hysteresis/cooldown, automatic rollback on regression, deterministic seeded/snapshot-versioned config, and a hard kill-switch to a known-good static configuration.
- **[research, M0]** RESEARCH: Define the advisor objective metric (throughput-per-core/memory, not raw hit ratio). Following Baleen's 'optimize the real cost metric' lesson and the hit-ratio-vs-throughput result, define and validate the advisor's reward/objective so it does not chase hit ratio at the expense of IronCache's stated throughput-per-core and memory-efficiency goals.
